Languages, Dialects, and Labels

Over here at sunny (and dry) Wordset headquarters, we've been quietly chugging away working on our Wordset 2.0 milestones. These changes aren't about big visual updates to the site or new buttons to hit everywhere. What we're doing is setting the stage for Wordset to equitably and fairly start cataloguing language. That's why we're adding support for multiple languages, dialect tagging, and usage labels.

Most of the code now supports multiple languages. You may have noticed that we recently changed the URLs of words from "/word/sushi" to "/en/sushi", so that we can provide the same website, but also eventually handle all the language codes we want. There are still some technical edge cases to work out on the site, but the real thing we need to launch in our second language is volunteers! We, unfortunately, only speak English natively, so we need native and fluent speakers of other languages to help do the hard work of getting a first version going. If you know someone who would be willing to help, we have a quick form about contributing that we'd love for you to fill out!

Dialects are important to us. One of the founders speaks British English, the other American English, and so we can't just go around making a dictionary that only supports one of those! But we also know that language is far more varied than just the two major branches of English. Labels are super easy to propose on meanings right now... just click the edit meaning button and select what labels you would like to apply.
